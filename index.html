Biography

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was a renowned Indian scientist and the 11th President of India. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. Kalam was a pioneer in aerospace engineering and played a crucial role in India's space and missile development.

Achievements

Dr. Kalam made significant contributions to India's space research and development programs, including the successful launch of the Pokhran-II nuclear tests. He received numerous awards and honors throughout his career, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award.
